About Van Gogh

Vincent Willem van Gogh (1853–1890) was a Dutch post-impressionist painter who became one of the most influential figures in the history of Western art. His paintings are known for their bold colors, emotional depth, and expressive brushwork. Van Gogh created more than 2,100 artworks, including about 860 oil paintings, many of which are considered masterpieces today. Despite his prolific work, he struggled with mental health issues and only gained recognition after his death.

Famous Works

Some of Van Gogh's most renowned paintings include:

Starry Night (1889)

This iconic painting depicts a night sky swirling with energy, symbolizing the emotional turmoil Van Gogh experienced during his time in an asylum in Saint-Rémy-de-Provence.

Sunflowers (1888)

Van Gogh's series of sunflower paintings are vibrant and symbolic of friendship. He painted them in Arles as a tribute to his friend Paul Gauguin.

Irises (1889)

Created during Van Gogh's stay in a mental hospital, this painting shows the vivid beauty of irises, reflecting his love for nature and its healing power.

Van Gogh's Legacy

Despite his struggles with mental health, Van Gogh’s work has had a profound influence on modern art. His emotive and expressive use of color and brushstroke techniques paved the way for Expressionism and Abstract art. Today, his works are held in major museums worldwide, and his life story continues to inspire artists and art enthusiasts.
